Women in Technology Java Programming Boot Camp will be held in-person from:
Friday, January 3, 2025 - Saturday, January 4, 2025.

The purpose of this boot camp is to:

  • Prepare students to succeed in Java Programming courses
    • Group for students who have been exposed to Java and want a refresher to prepare for other IT classes
  • Enable women in IT to form a community of support with peers, student mentors, and IT faculty
  • Provide professional development opportunities such as guest speakers and off-campus field trips

Non-Discrimination Statement

The Women in Technology (WIT) Java Programming Boot Camp is designed to address the underrepresentation of women in technology. However, participation is open to all students regardless of gender, and we encourage anyone interested in enhancing their programming skills to apply.

Clarification of Grant Purpose

This boot camp is supported by a Google grant aimed at providing opportunities for underrepresented groups in IT. While the program seeks to encourage women in technology, it welcomes all students to apply.

Equal Access to Benefits

All participants, regardless of gender, will receive equal access to programming instruction, professional development opportunities, guest speaker sessions, certificates, and other resources. The goal of the boot camp is to foster an inclusive and supportive learning environment for everyone.

Title IX Compliance

In compliance with Title IX, which prohibits gender-based discrimination in educational programs receiving federal funds, this boot camp is designed to offer equal opportunities to all participants, regardless of gender. We are committed to ensuring that no student is excluded from participation or denied benefits based on gender.

Fee Waiver Information

Registration for non-GGC students includes a $25 fee. Fee waivers may be available for eligible non-GGC students based on academic merit (e.g., GPA of 3.3 or higher). Eligibility will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis, and all students meeting the criteria are encouraged to apply. This ensures that waivers are offered equitably to all eligible participants, regardless of gender.

Inclusive Marketing

While this boot camp focuses on supporting women in technology, all marketing and promotional materials reflect the inclusive nature of the program. We welcome and encourage students of all genders to participate in the program and benefit from the learning and professional development opportunities it offers.
